Section 3

Cohort Retention Analysis

Cohort analysis tracks groups of customers acquired in the same month and measures how many return in subsequent months. This reveals the platform's true customer stickiness beyond headline order numbers.

Figure 4 — Monthly cohort retention heatmap (%). Darker = higher retention. Empty cells = insufficient data.

🔴 Critical Finding: Low Repeat Purchase Rate

Month-1 retention averages just ~5% across cohorts — meaning 95% of customers do not return the month after their first purchase. This is a structural platform challenge.

📉 Retention Stabilizes at ~0.3–0.5%

After the initial drop-off, a small loyal core (0.3–0.5%) continues to purchase each month — suggesting a genuine repeat buyer segment exists but needs nurturing.

💡 Loyalty Program Opportunity

The sharp drop after month 0 signals that a structured post-purchase re-engagement flow (email, loyalty points, discount codes) could meaningfully improve retention and LTV.

Strategic Recommendations

Data-Driven Action Plan

Based on the analysis, here are six prioritized recommendations for Olist's growth and retention strategy:

  • 🎯
    Launch a Post-Purchase Re-Engagement Campaign With Month-1 retention at ~5%, a 7/14/30-day email sequence with personalized product recommendations and first-reorder discounts could significantly lift LTV. Target: Recent Customers and Potential Loyalists segments first.
  • 🔴
    Activate "At-Risk" & "Can't Lose Them" Winback Programs These two segments represent R$ 5.8M in historical revenue from 30,900 customers. Deploy a tiered win-back offer (discount + free shipping) within 90 days of last purchase before they cross the 180-day churn threshold.
  • 🏆
    Invest in Champions Loyalty Perks 14,961 Champions generate R$ 2.65M. A VIP program (early access, exclusive discounts, priority support) costs little but signals recognition — reducing the risk of losing your most valuable customers to competitors.
  • 🚚
    Reduce Delivery Variance to Protect Review Scores High delivery time variance (std dev > 8 days) is the most likely driver of 1-star reviews. Partnering with regional fulfillment centers or flagging high-risk routes for proactive customer communication could improve NPS and repeat purchase rates.
  • 📦
    Expand High-AOV Categories (Watches, Computers) Watches/Gifts and Computers/Accessories have the highest average order values. Targeted paid search and affiliate marketing in these verticals would grow revenue without proportionally growing order volume.
  • 🗺️
    Geographic Expansion into Brazil's North & Northeast States like AM, CE, and PE are severely underrepresented. Subsidized logistics or regional seller recruitment could open a largely untapped market with 50M+ potential customers.
Methodology & Tech Stack

How This Analysis Was Built

🛠️ Tools & Libraries

Python 3.11 · Pandas · NumPy · Matplotlib · Seaborn · Scikit-learn · Jupyter Notebooks

📐 RFM Methodology

Quintile scoring (1–5) on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary dimensions. Snapshot date = max purchase + 1 day. 8-segment rule-based classification.

📊 Cohort Method

Acquisition cohort = customer's first purchase month. Cohort index = months elapsed since acquisition. Retention % = returning unique customers / initial cohort size.

🔍 Data Quality

96,478 of 99,441 orders (97%) had "delivered" status and were used. Payments were aggregated per order. Missing geolocation data was excluded.
